Okonomiyaki

Description

A popular street food from Osaka, Okonomiyaki is a delicious Japanese savory pancake “grilled as you like it“ with your choice of protein and tasty condiments and toppings. My recipe includes the 6 key ingredients that give your Okonomiyaki a truly authentic taste.


Ingredients

    Serves 2

  • 240g all-purpose flour
  • 0.5 tsp baking powder
  • 0.5 tsp salt
  • 0.5 tsp sugar
  • 360ml dashi
  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 lettuce/cabbge
  • 500g king prawns
  • Okonomiyaki sauce
  • Kewpie mayo
  • Parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour, salt, sugar, baking powder and mix well together.
  2. Add dashi into the bowl.
  3. Mix it all together until combined. Cover the bowl with cling film and set in the fridge for at least an hour. Tip: Resting the batter relaxes the gluten, improves the flavor, and makes the okonomiyaki fluffier. Some okonomiyaki shops refrigerate the batter overnight.
  4. Mince the cabbage.
  5. After one hour of setting, take the batter out from the fridge. Add large eggs into bowl and mix well.
  6. Add minced cabbage into the bowl 1/3 at a time. Mix well before adding the rest.
  7. Cut each of the king prawns in half. Add into the bowl and mix well.
  8. In a large pan, heat oil on medium heat. When the frying pan is hot (400ºF or 200ºC), spread the batter in a circle on the pan. We like thicker okonomiyaki (the final thickness is ¾ inches or 2 cm). Cover to cook for 5 minutes.
  9. When the bottom side is nicely browned, flip it over.
  10. Gently press the okonomiyaki to fix its shape and keep it together. Cover and cook for another 5 minutes.
  11. Flip it over one last time and cook uncovered for 2 minutes.
  12. Transfer okonomiyaki to plate. Spread okonokiyaki sauce well across the okonokiyaki. Garnish with Kewpie mayo and parsley.
